Overview of the Indian Constitution

The Constitution of India is the supreme law of the land, laying down the framework that defines the political principles, structure, procedures, powers, and duties of government institutions. It also enshrines fundamental rights, directive principles, and the duties of citizens.

Key Features:

  • Federal Structure: The Constitution establishes a federal structure with a strong central government and distribution of powers between the central and state governments.
  • Fundamental Rights: Guarantees individual rights such as equality before the law, freedom of speech, and protection against discrimination.
  • Directive Principles: Guidelines for the state to ensure economic and social justice, although they are not justiciable.
  • Amendment Process: Provides a mechanism for making changes to the Constitution to adapt to evolving needs and circumstances.
  • Separation of Powers: Divides government powers among the executive, legislature, and judiciary to ensure checks and balances.
  • Emergency Provisions: Allows for the declaration of national, state, and financial emergencies to address exceptional situations.
